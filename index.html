Favorite Reads of 2023...so far

Being Mortal: Medicine and What Matters in the End by Atul Gawande

Gawande covers great topics about end of life and how extraordinary measures near the end of life often do more harm than good. Instead of focusing on happiness in the final years of an illness or old age, people consume endless treatments and trials in an attempt to prolong life. By refocusing and considering quality over quantity, people can instead enjoy the last few years with their loved ones.

What My Bones Know by Stephanie Foo

This memoir explores abuse, intergenerational trauma, and self-acceptance. Foo explores the journey through learning to move forward in spite of her experienced abuse in childhood. She discovers that the way that she was raised stemmed from her parents also experiencing a lot of tough situations. In order to move forward, we all have to do some form of work in forgiving and learning about ourselves.

Even though I don't believe I have experienced traumatic events in life, there are habits and perceptions that were passed down and learned from my parents.
